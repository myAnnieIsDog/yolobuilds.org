The following are frequently cited code sections during structural plan review and inspection, including several triggers for engineered design.

Prescriptive Requirements

2022 California Residential Code
Conventional Light-Frame Wood Construction

General

Deviations

[R301.1.3.1]
When any portion of any structure deviates from substantial compliance with conventional framing requirements for woodframe construction found in this code, the building official shall require the construction documents to be approved and stamped by a California licensed architect or engineer for that irregular or non-conforming portion of work.

More than Two Stories

[R301.1.3.2 and R301.2.2.7]
The building official shall require construction documents to be approved and stamped by a California licensed architect or engineer for all dwellings of woodframe construction more than two stories and basement in height.

Other Than Wood Frame

[R301.1.3.3]
The building official shall require floor, wall or roof-ceiling structural elements in dwellings designed of cold-formed steel, concrete, masonry or structural insulated panels prescribed by this code to be approved and stamped by a California licensed architect or engineer.

Shipping Containers

[R301.1.4]
Intermodal shipping containers that are repurposed for use as buildings or structures shall be designed in accordance with the structural provisions in Section 3115 of the California Building Code.

Wind Speed

[R301.2.1]
Ultimate design wind speed is 93 mph.

Seismic Design Category

[R301.2.2]
Use Seismic Design Category D2 or engineering site analysis.

Dead Loads

[R301.2.2.2]
Provide engineered design if assembly dead load exceeds the following:

  • 15 psf Roof-Ceiling Assembly
  • 10 psf Floor Assembly
  • 15 psf Exterior light-frame walls
  • 10psf Interior light-frame walls

Irregularities

[R301.2.2.6]
The following irregularities require engineered design [see exceptions]:

  • Shear wall or braced wall offsets out of plane.
  • Floor or roof is not laterally supported by shear walls or braced wall lines on all edges.
  • Shear wall or braced wall offsets in plane.
  • Floor or roof opening exceeds 12 ft or 50% of the dimension.
  • Portions of a floor level are vertically offset.
  • Where shear wall and braced wall lines do not occur in two perpendicular directions.
  • Where above grade story is wood-framed plus concrete or masonry construction.
  • Where slope exceeds 1V:5H and the tallest cripple wall or post and beam exceeds 7 ft.

Plumbing Fixtures

[R301.2.2.10]
Water heaters and thermal storage units shall be anchored per Plumbing Code.

Story Height

[R301.]
Story heights exceeding 11-ft 7-in require engineered design.

Treated Wood

[R317.1]
Protection of wood against decay shall be provided at the following locations:

  • Where floor joists are within 18" of exposed ground.
  • Where floor girders are within 12" of exposed ground.
  • Where floor columns are within 8" of exposed ground.
  • Where sills and sleepers are supported by concrete.
  • Where siding/sheathing are within 6” of ground or within 2” of concrete flatwork.
  • Where balconies, porches and similar supports are exposed to weather.
  • Field cuts, notches, and holes shall be treated in the field.

Balconies

[R317.1.3]
Enclosed framing in exterior balconies and elevated walking surfaces that are exposed to rain shall be provided with opening that provide a net-free cross-ventilation area not less than 1/150 of the area of each separate space.
