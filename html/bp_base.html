Abandoned Applications

An application for a permit for any proposed work shall be deemed to have been abandoned 180 days after the date of filing, unless such application has been pursued in good faith. A permit holder may request in writing to extend an unexpired permit for up to 180 days if justifiable cause is demonstrated.

Expired Permits

Every issued permit remains valid if the work on the site authorized by that permit is commenced within 12 months after its issuance, unless the permittee has abandoned the work authorized by the permit. A permit holder may request in writing to extend an unexpired permit for up to 180 days if justifiable cause is demonstrated.

Reinstate Permit

If a permit is abandoned or expired, a new permit application must be submitted. The fees will be the hourly rates to review the application under the current regulations up to a maximum of one-half the fee for a new permit.

Code Enforcement

If the permit is the result of a code enforcement case, then allowing a permit to be abandoned or expire is noncompliance and an administrative citation will be issued. Any timelines stated in a Courtesy Notice or Administrative Citation take precedence over the general timelines listed above.

{% block additional_requirements %}

For pool demolition, also specify the following code requirements:

  • If the pool is not completely removed, specify that a minimum of eight (8) holes with a minimum diameter of 8-inches will be drilled completely through the bottom so water will be able to drain.
  • If the pool is not completely removed the top must either remain above grade or remove the top 24 inches of the bond beam.
  • The pool shall be backfilled and compacted to a minimum of ninety percent (90%) maximum density. No organic or other reducible material shall be incorporated in fills. No rock or similar irreducible material with a maximum dimension greater than (8) inches shall be buried or placed within forty-eight (48) inches of finished grade. Field density shall be determined by a method acceptable to the Building Official.

Plan Review Checklist

The Building Division staff will review the following. Any other hazards identified by staff will also have to be adequately addressed by the applicant.

  • Verify ownership of the property.
  • Verify the dimensions of the structure are shown on the plan and match any known data such as permit recordds, GIS images, or assessor's records.
  • Verify the area of the structure based on the dimensions.
  • Verify the correct area is used on fee calculations and impact fee forms.
    • {% block review_requirements %}
  • Verify the utilities are specified to be disconnected and properly terminated.
  • Verify all agencies have approved the demolition before stamping and signing the construction documents.
  • For pool demolition or other underground structures, verify the method of removal, backfill, and compaction meets all requirements.
  • For partial demolition, the plans must clearly indicate which portions of the structure will be demolished and which portions will remain. Detailed plans may be required.
